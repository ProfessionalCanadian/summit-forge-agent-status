## Art Style Decision — Profile Pictures

**Current leading style: Premium Playing Card Illustration (pending Josh's confirmation)**

Rationale: Summit Forge Labs produces physical luxury playing card decks as one of their products. The illustration style — black background, gold/silver linework, ornate court card composition — is directly on-brand and references their own product line. Two test images generated (Oscar as King, Sentinel as Knight) and sent to Josh for approval.

Previous directions tested and rejected:
- Stylized 3D Render / Chrome Androids (Westworld aesthetic) — Josh: "super cool but doesn't match the brand"
- Animal-themed characters — rejected earlier

**Per-agent states (4 images each):**
1. **Awake** — alert, neutral, ready
2. **Working** — focused, determined, engaged
3. **Sleeping/Dreaming** — eyes closed, relaxed, subtle glow
4. **Degraded/Poor health** — visually stressed, glitching, fragmented

**Shared style prompt prefix (all agents):**
> "Stylized 3D render, cinematic lighting, chrome and steel aesthetic, dark background, premium character portrait, Summit Forge Labs industrial-tech visual language, [agent-specific details]"

## UI Design Language

- Font: Inter (system fallback)
- Tile shape: rounded rectangle, 8px radius
- Depth: subtle box-shadow with a silver/chrome highlight on top edge
- Motion: status badge pulse, smooth hover lift
- Logo placement: header left, 48px height
- Background texture: subtle noise or faint gear pattern (optional)

## Business Context (updated 2026-04-09)

**Summit Forge Labs is a fabrication and 3D printing studio.**

- Based in Calgary, AB (#213 2949 17 Ave SE, Calgary AB, 403-902-2175)
- Serves construction industry clients (Soletanche Bachy Canada, AGRA, Western Caissons confirmed)
- Products include: 3D printed branded models, custom playing card decks, physical signage/plaques
- "Forge" is literal — they make physical things with precision
- "Labs" is the workshop/studio

**Brand implication:** The aesthetic should honor physical, tactile craft.
Illustration styles should feel like they could be embossed, engraved, or printed on a premium physical product.
The playing card deck is not just a reference — it IS one of their products.
